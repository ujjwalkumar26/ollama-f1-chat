Formula 1, often abbreviated as F1, is the highest class of international racing for single-seater formula cars. It is sanctioned by the Fédération Internationale de l’Automobile (FIA). The championship began in 1950 and has since evolved into one of the most technologically advanced and globally followed motorsport events.

The 2023 Formula One season featured major rivalries between Red Bull Racing, Mercedes, and Ferrari. Max Verstappen, driving for Red Bull, dominated the season, breaking numerous records for wins and points. His teammate Sergio Pérez also performed strongly, securing multiple podium finishes.

Lewis Hamilton, driving for Mercedes, continued to chase his record-breaking eighth world championship title. Although Mercedes struggled with car balance and porpoising issues due to new ground-effect regulations introduced in 2022, they made steady improvements over the year.

Ferrari, with drivers Charles Leclerc and Carlos Sainz, showed flashes of brilliance but continued to suffer from strategic mis-steps and reliability issues. The team’s car, the SF-23, was competitive in qualifying but often lacked race pace compared to Red Bull’s RB19.

McLaren, after a slow start to the season, found significant mid-season upgrades that propelled Lando Norris and rookie Oscar Piastri into contention for podiums. Their aerodynamic improvements and efficient tyre management were praised throughout the paddock.

Aston Martin emerged as a surprise contender early in the 2023 season, largely due to Fernando Alonso’s experience and the AMR23’s strong design inspired by Red Bull’s aerodynamic philosophy. Alonso consistently finished in the top five, earning several podiums and rejuvenating the team’s reputation.

Alpine, formerly Renault, had an inconsistent year. Esteban Ocon and Pierre Gasly occasionally achieved strong results but the team struggled to maintain consistent development progress.

The 2023 F1 calendar featured 23 races, including returning venues such as the Qatar Grand Prix and the Las Vegas Grand Prix. The Las Vegas race, held on the iconic Strip, was one of the most anticipated events in F1 history due to its unique night-time setting and spectacular visuals.

F1 cars in the 2023 era use 1.6-liter V6 turbo-hybrid power units. Each team designs its chassis but must adhere to strict FIA regulations on aerodynamics, safety, and cost cap rules. The cost cap, introduced to make the sport more sustainable, limited team spending to about US$135 million per season (excluding driver salaries and marketing costs).

Pirelli is the sole tyre supplier, offering compounds ranging from C0 to C5. Tyre strategy plays a crucial role in every race, as pit stops, degradation, and undercuts often decide the outcome.

The role of data and artificial intelligence in F1 has grown significantly. Teams use machine learning for predictive modelling, race simulations, and optimising strategies. AI-driven analytics help engineers understand tyre wear patterns, aerodynamic efficiency, and energy recovery system (ERS) usage.

Formula 1’s governing body, the FIA, continues to push sustainability goals. Synthetic fuels, carbon-neutral operations, and electric power integration are core targets for 2030. The sport also emphasises fan engagement through social media, virtual reality experiences, and the popular Netflix series “Drive to Survive.”

Key F1 terminology:  
- DRS (Drag Reduction System): A mechanism that reduces aerodynamic drag on straights to aid overtaking.  
- ERS (Energy Recovery System): A hybrid component that stores energy from braking and exhaust gases for additional power.  
- Undercut: A pit stop strategy to gain track position by pitting earlier than a rival.  
- Overcut: Staying out longer to take advantage of better track position or fresher tyres.  
- Slipstream: The aerodynamic benefit of following closely behind another car to reduce air resistance.
